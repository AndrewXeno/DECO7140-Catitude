About Catitude

About Jackson

Jackson Supernova, a cat behaviourist with more than fifteen years of experience, Jackson Supernova specializes in helping individuals whose "out of control" cats are turning their lives and homes upside down.

At Catitude Clinic we have a licensed veterinarian and a cat behaviourist with certification (associate certified by the IAABC) and formal study in cat behaviour that take the time to practice responsible and ethical behaviour care for cats. We offer regular and drop in consultation appointments to provide you and your cat(s) an effective and all-encompassing behaviour plan. Dedicated to the study of cat behaviour, Catitude conducts behaviour studies as part of many of our behaviour consultations. This invaluable information benefits not only the consulting cat and owner, but also the lives of cats everywhere. Supernova has studied feline and animal behaviour for 20 years, conducted behaviour studies, and most recent study of animal behaviour was at The University of Queensland. He travels worldwide to lecture on cat behaviour (most recently as the keynote speaker for the Humane Society and SPCA in Toronto, Canada behaviour convention 2014) and in addition to working with the Animal Welfare League internationally, has authored a scholarly cited and science-based cat behaviour book (Random House Publishing) with the latest strategies to help cats - as opposed to other cat behaviour books filled with obsolete and incomplete advice you can easily find on the internet.

Many people will tell you that it's impossible to train or otherwise change the behaviour of a cat. But over the last two decades our behaviourists have successfully applied a method consisting of compassionate awareness and three-part behaviour modification to help resolve the unwanted behaviours of thousands of cats, and we're here to show cat lovers that cats' behaviour can be reliably and consistently changed for the better. And we'll show you exactly how using the uniquely effective three-stage method of solving every one of the seven major cat behaviour problems.

Our Philosophy: Cats are not like dogs. They are also not like humans. All cat behaviour consulting has to start with this awareness. That's because nearly all failed efforts to modify cats' behaviour assume that cat psychology is like dog psychology (or worse, like human psychology). These efforts are doomed to fail because they are based on ignorance of the uniqueness of individual cats and instinctual colony behaviour dynamics. Cat behaviour specialist Jackson Supernova is here to help you.
